
      ‘It’s a new era’: Weight-loss treatments significantly lower blood pressure, studies find
    

Two studies showed significant blood pressure reductions in adults undergoing weight loss treatments: one with weekly drug injections and the other with bariatric surgery.

The research highlights the potential benefits of treating obesity for overall heart health.

These findings suggest a new era in obesity treatment, emphasizing the impact of weight loss on reducing heart disease risks.

However, cost and insurance coverage may limit access to these new treatments, potentially exacerbating health disparities in the US.
