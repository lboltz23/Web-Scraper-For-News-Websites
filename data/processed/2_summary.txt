
      ‘It’s a new era’: Weight-loss treatments significantly lower blood pressure, studies find
    

Two new studies show significant reductions in blood pressure for adults undergoing weight-loss treatments: weekly injections of the drug tirzepatide and bariatric surgery.

The studies provide evidence that treating obesity effectively can improve overall heart health.

This new era of anti-obesity medications and surgery offers profound benefits and potential improvements in cardiovascular conditions.

However, barriers like cost and insurance coverage may limit access to these treatments and worsen health disparities.
